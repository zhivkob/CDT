const TriangleVec &  triangles,
const EdgeUSet &  fixedEdges

Calculate depth of each triangle in constraint triangulation.

Perform depth peeling from super triangle to outermost boundary, then to next boundary and so on until all triangles are traversed. For example depth is:

  • 0 for triangles outside outermost boundary
  • 1 for triangles inside boundary but outside hole
  • 2 for triangles in hole
  • 3 for triangles in island and so on...
Parameters
seedseed triangle to begin depth-peeling from
trianglestriangles of triangulation
fixedEdgesconstraint edges of triangulation
Returns
vector where element at index i stores depth of i-th triangle

Depth-peel a layer in triangulation, used when calculating triangle depths.

It takes starting seed triangles, traverses neighboring triangles, and assigns given layer depth to the traversed triangles. Traversal is blocked by constraint edges. Triangles behind constraint edges are recorded as seeds of next layer and returned from the function.

Template Parameters
Ttype of vertex coordinates (e.g., float, double)
Parameters
seedsindices of seed triangles
trianglestriangles of triangulation
fixedEdgesconstraint edges of triangulation
layerDepthcurrent layer's depth to mark triangles with
[in,out]triDepthsdepths of triangles
Returns
triangles of the next (deeper) layer that are adjacent to the peeled layer. To be used as seeds when peeling the next layer.

  • each triangle's neighbor in turn has triangle as its neighbor
